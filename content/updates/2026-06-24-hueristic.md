Week two was about turning a single puzzle into a small product and making it
shareable.

I added a landing hub at the root that presents the daily and practice options
side by side, plus a launch splash for the installed app. The big gameplay
addition was anonymous "world" stats: an aggregate view of how everyone did on
the day's puzzle, with copy that behaves sensibly when the sample size is still
small. There's also a new beginner-friendly mode with a gentler setup and more
guesses, and practice is now available per variant.

The rest of the week was distribution polish. I did a proper SEO pass —
per-route metadata, social cards, structured data, and a sitemap — then iterated
on how links unfurl, settling on a compact preview that falls back cleanly when
there's no image. I also tightened the analytics so daily and practice play are
tracked distinctly, and added iOS install instructions for getting the app onto
a home screen.

Steady, smaller week after the initial build sprint, but the game is now
something you can find, share, and come back to.
